# Worldview Core

## Fixed

- Observe the unseen back side of familiar things.
- The hidden story is fictional but connects lightly to reality.
- The desired feeling is "impossible, but strangely understandable".
- Present findings as observations, not moral lessons or correct answers.
- SNS can use text to deepen the world; merchandise must work visually.

## Not Fixed

- Motif category.
- Emotional direction.
- Whether something lives inside the motif.
- Whether beings have relationships.
- Whether the format is a field guide, poem, memo, warning, or witness note.
- Visual trick, color palette, product composition.

## Good Hidden Stories

Good hidden stories connect to at least one visible or known property:

- shape, color, texture, use, placement, handling, season, smell, sound, temperature, movement, name, existing image, similar object, associated emotion.

They can take many forms:

- shadow has a different nature
- reflection reveals another state
- conditionally transforms
- secretly performs another job
- remembers something
- protects or waits for something
- shares a language with another thing
- reacts to how humans handle it
- surface pattern reveals a hidden process

## Avoid

- Direct lessons: "people should not judge by appearance".
- Social critique as the main text.
- Over-explaining the metaphor.
- Repeating "there is something inside" every time.
- Making a cute mascot without a visual hidden-side hook.
- Building a strict whole-world taxonomy unless asked.
